Our Industry

THERE IS A LARGE GAP BETWEEN WHERE COMPANIES' HR DEPARTMENTS ARE AND WHERE THEY WANT TO BE.
MY CABINET WILL BE THE LEADING HR TECHNOLOGY COMPANY BECAUSE WE ARE COMMITTED TO BRIDGING THE GAP

Inception of the contemporary Human Resource department, then formalized as the “Industrial and Labor Relations” department, began in the 20th Century. The arena of Labor relations was created through the exploration of ways to create business value through the strategic management of workers. Consequently, the functions were dominated by transactional work – minimizing cost and reducing risk.

PAST

There has been growth in corporate culture, as companies are beginning to view employees as assets rather than cost of goods within a larger machine. However, progression has been too slow, causing feelings of disenchantment for corporate ideals of work/ life balance among millennials. Thus, companies lack loyal and committed workers because of their insufficient engagement, recruitment, and retainment strategies. 57% of companies plan to invest in HR technology in the coming year because they recognize this deficit.

PRESENT

Millennials are projected to be 75% of workforce by 2025. Thus, 2016 and beyond must be seen as a time for creativity, bold leadership, and a fundamental reimagining of the practices HR leaders have used for years. Organizations must rethink their overall approach to handling talent. There must be disruptions within the HR department as this department will ultimately shift the culture of the entire organization. This will be achieved through the My Cabinet approach – add transparency, incorporate efficiency, effectiveness and the internalization of the mantra “It’s not a Job, It’s a lifestyle!”
